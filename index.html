About Dr Daniel Shmukler

A dedicated consultant anaesthetist with a special interest in neuro-anaesthesia, committed to providing personalised, evidence-based care in Johannesburg.

Professional Background

Dr Daniel Shmukler is a highly experienced consultant anaesthetist practicing in Johannesburg, South Africa. With over 15 years of clinical experience, he has developed a reputation for excellence in perioperative care, particularly in the field of neuro-anaesthesia for brain and spinal surgery.

After completing his medical degree at the University of the Witwatersrand, Dr Shmukler pursued specialised training in anaesthetics, earning his Fellowship of the College of Anaesthetists. His commitment to continuous learning has led him to develop particular expertise in neuro-anaesthesia, regional anaesthesia, and advanced monitoring techniques.

Dr Shmukler's approach to patient care is centred on clear communication, evidence-based practice, and individualised treatment plans. He believes that understanding your anaesthetic care reduces anxiety and improves outcomes, which is why he takes time to explain procedures and answer questions thoroughly.

Anaesthetic Services

Comprehensive anaesthetic care tailored to your specific needs, from routine procedures to complex neurosurgical operations. Every service is delivered with the highest standards of safety and patient comfort.

General Anaesthesia

Complete unconsciousness for major surgical procedures with comprehensive monitoring and safety protocols.

Suitable For

  • Major abdominal, thoracic, or orthopaedic surgery
  • Procedures requiring muscle relaxation
  • Operations lasting longer than 2 hours

Regional & Nerve Blocks

Targeted anaesthesia using local anaesthetic injection to block specific nerves, providing excellent pain relief.

Suitable For

  • Limb surgery (arms, legs, hands, feet)
  • Shoulder, knee, and hip procedures
  • Post-operative pain management

Neuro-anaesthesia

Specialised anaesthetic management for brain and spinal surgery with advanced monitoring and techniques.

Suitable For

  • Brain tumour removal and craniotomies
  • Spinal fusion and complex spinal surgery
  • Awake craniotomy procedures

Patient Information

Everything you need to know to prepare for your procedure and ensure a smooth recovery. Please read through these guidelines carefully and don't hesitate to ask questions.

Pre-operative Preparation

Following these instructions carefully helps ensure your safety and the success of your procedure.

Pre-operative Checklist

1

Fast from food for 6 hours before surgery (clear fluids up to 2 hours before)

2

Take regular medications with small sips of water unless instructed otherwise

3

Arrange reliable transport home (no driving for 24 hours after anaesthetic)

4

Remove all jewellery, contact lenses, and nail polish

5

Bring a list of all current medications and allergies

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about anaesthesia and what to expect during your care.

Stop eating solid foods 6 hours before surgery. You may have clear fluids (water, black tea, black coffee) up to 2 hours before surgery. This includes no milk, cream, or chewing gum.

With general anaesthesia, you will be completely unconscious and feel nothing. With regional anaesthesia, the surgical area will be completely numb. We monitor your comfort throughout and can provide additional medication if needed.
